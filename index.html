Who are we?

The Aeromodelling Club at our college is a vibrant community of aviation enthusiasts who come together to learn, design, build, and fly both model airplanes and drones. The club members work on designing and building various types of model airplanes, from basic gliders to advanced models that mimic real-world aircraft. They also develop and operate drones, including quadcopters and fixed-wing drones equipped with cameras and other sensors. The club's activities provide an opportunity for students to learn about the principles of flight and navigation and apply this knowledge to create advanced drone systems and airplane models. In addition to participating in drone racing competitions and using drones for aerial photography and videography, the club members also showcase their airplane models at intercollegiate competitions and exhibitions. The Aeromodelling Club is an excellent platform for students to pursue their passion for aviation and technology and to connect with like-minded individuals who share their interests.

How to Join us?

Everyone who is enthusiastic about making planes and drones and loves to understand their aerodynamics is welcome aboard. You can sign up for any project of your wish during the project distribution every year. Not only is our team confined to working on aerial vehicles we also have a dedicated Events and outreach team as well as a Content and Media team. These teams are recruited every year through a simple interview process.
